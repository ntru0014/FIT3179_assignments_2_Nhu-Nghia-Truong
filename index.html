Tourism in Australia before Covid 19

“Discovering with a Australia’s tourism story”

FIT3179 Assignment 2 - Nhu Nghia Truong

Australia’s tourism story is one of energy, growth, and resilience. These visuals bring that story to life — showing how millions of visitors explored the country, how long they stayed, and how the industry thrived before the world changed in 2020. When the borders closed, everything slowed down — but the data reminds us just how powerful travel can be for people, places, and the economy. Through these charts, we see not just numbers, but the heartbeat of Australia’s tourism journey.

Short-term visitor arrivals in Australia from 2014 to 2020

Stack line chart and line graph display short-term tourist arrivals Australia trend sorted by lengths of stay and purposes.

Interaction: Use the selector for line chart to choose visitor purposes. Hover over data to gain numbers.
Graph one: Visitor in Australia by Length of Stay (2014 - 2020)

Visitor numbers rose steadily from 2014 to 2019, showing strong tourism growth before COVID-19.

The most common stay was between 2 weeks and 3 months, typical of leisure and long-haul travellers.

All categories dropped sharply in 2020, from around 9 millions to 2 millions.

~

Graph two: Visitor Intentions of Stay (2014 - 2020)

Total holiday visitors increased by around half from 2014 to 2019, indicating high demand growth.

2019 marked the peak year for arrivals, with over 4.5 million visitors to Australia.

Visitor numbers plunged to only ~789,000 in 2020, the lowest level recorded in decades.

Impact of COVID-19

COVID-19 caused an sharp decrease in international tourism, cutting arrivals by more than 80%.

The data shows how heavily reliant Australia was on overseas holidaymakers and highlights the sector's vulnerability to global travel restrictions.

Tourism Satellite Account

These graphs display the output and gross value added to Australian GDP by tourism-related industries in millions (AUD). Data shows year 2016 to 2020.

Output is the total profit that were recored for each industry, each year, while Gross Value Added (GVA) is the revenue that added to Australia GDP each year.

Interaction: Hover over data to gain more insight of each industries.
Graph 1: Tourism Output by Industries (2016 - 2020)

Tourism output peaked in 2018 at around 100 billion AUD, showing strong pre-COVID growth.

Accommodation, food services, and transport were the largest contributors throughout the period.

Output dropped by nearly half in 2020 (roughly 59 billion AUD, see graph) as international and domestic travel halted.

~

Graph 2: Tourism Gross Value Added (GVA) by Industries (2016 - 2020)

GVA followed a similar trend, reaching a high in 2018 before a sharp fall in 2020.

Accommodation and restaurants remained core value drivers, while education and transport also played significant roles.

2020 GVA declined to about 26 billion AUD, reflecting massive lossed due to COVID-19 restrictions.

Impact of COVID-19

COVID-19 caused the steepest contraction in tourism's economic contribution across both output and GVA.

The downturn highlights the industry's dependence on travel and hospitality activities
